## 1. Positioning Brief

### Who It Is For

Israeli DTC (direct-to-consumer) founders and growth managers running Shopify stores. Specifically: brands doing meaningful volume (hundreds of orders per month), managing COGS across multiple SKUs, running paid campaigns (Meta/Instagram), and drowning in disconnected tools — Shopify admin, Meta Ads Manager, Google Analytics, spreadsheets — none of which talk to each other in real time, and none of which speak Hebrew.

### The Pain

Israeli DTC brands operate at a structural disadvantage relative to their US counterparts: the best analytics tools (Triple Whale, Northbeam, Polar Analytics) are built for American or European brands, priced in USD, supported in English only, and designed around market conditions (ROAS benchmarks, tax treatment, LTV assumptions) that do not map to the Israeli market. The result is that Israeli founders either overpay for tools not built for them or make decisions from incomplete, manually assembled data.

The deeper pain: founders do not have time to be analysts. They need a weekly, human-readable summary in Hebrew that tells them what happened, what changed, and what to do next — not another dashboard to log into.

### The Unique Angle

Brandzp Analytics is the first Hebrew-native Shopify analytics SaaS built by an Israeli founder for Israeli DTC brands. It combines real profitability (true gross profit per SKU, COGS-adjusted contribution margin), retention cohorts, affiliate attribution, and a weekly AI-generated founder digest — delivered in Hebrew, via email, every Monday morning.

The competitive moat is not a feature. It is language, context, and trust: a tool built by someone who understands Israeli e-commerce, Israeli tax context, and Israeli founder psychology.

### Key Differentiators

| Dimension | Brandzp Analytics | Triple Whale | Polar Analytics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Language | Hebrew-first, bilingual | English only | English only |
| Market focus | Israeli DTC | US-centric | EU-centric |
| Pricing | Affordable (Starter/Growth), 14-day trial | $$$, enterprise-leaning | $$, mid-market |
| Weekly digest | Hebrew AI summary, emailed to founder | No equivalent | No equivalent |
| Profit view | COGS per SKU, true GM | Attribution-heavy | Attribution-heavy |
| Support | Local founder | US support team | EU support team |

## 2. Five Target Beta Brands

The following five Israeli DTC Shopify brands are realistic candidates for beta outreach. They represent categories where profitability visibility and multi-channel attribution are acutely painful, and where a Hebrew-language tool would provide immediate daily-use value.

### 1. Fox Home (פוקס הום)
- **Category:** Home goods / lifestyle
- **Why they'd benefit:** High SKU count across textiles, candles, and accessories means COGS-per-SKU visibility and contribution margin tracking are complex. Multi-channel (Meta + physical stores) attribution is a daily headache.
- **Estimated Shopify size:** Large

### 2. Laline (לאלין)
- **Category:** Beauty / personal care
- **Why they'd benefit:** Strong repeat-purchase business where cohort retention, LTV, and churn curves are the primary growth lever. A Hebrew weekly digest summarising retention trends would replace a full analyst hire.
- **Estimated Shopify size:** Large

### 3. Castro Fashion (קסטרו)
- **Category:** Apparel / fashion
- **Why they'd benefit:** Apparel has high SKU churn, seasonal COGS volatility, and significant refund complexity — exactly what the Profit page addresses. Heavy Meta spender who needs proper ROAS-to-gross-profit bridging.
- **Estimated Shopify size:** Large

### 4. Intimissimi Israel (אינטימיסימי ישראל)
- **Category:** Apparel / intimate apparel
- **Why they'd benefit:** Franchise operator of a European brand, selling locally through Shopify. Needs local-language reporting tools to translate store performance for Israeli management without relying on Italian HQ dashboards.
- **Estimated Shopify size:** Mid

### 5. Amaizin Israel / Israeli Organic DTC brands (e.g., Teva Naot Direct, GoodFoodCompany)
- **Category:** Food / organic / health
- **Why they'd benefit:** Food DTC brands in Israel operate on very tight margins where COGS accuracy is existential. The Profit page's SKU-level gross margin view is a direct answer to a category-specific pain. Smaller team = the weekly AI digest replaces the need for a dedicated analyst entirely.
- **Estimated Shopify size:** Small to Mid

**Note for owner:** If you have first-degree connections at any of these brands or friendlier small-brand equivalents in your network (e.g., a founder running a 200-500 orders/month clothing brand on Shopify), those connections will convert faster than cold outreach to the large brands above. The large brands are listed to demonstrate positioning strength; for beta, prioritise warm contacts.

## 4. Beta Onboarding Checklist

The following checklist is for the beta customer. It covers everything they need to do to be fully set up and get value in the first week.

---

**Hebrew first, English in parentheses:**

1. **צרו חשבון וחברו את חנות ה-Shopify שלכם** (Create your account and connect your Shopify store) — כנסו לאפליקציה, לחצו על "Connect Shopify", והתחברו דרך ה-OAuth של Shopify. הסנכרון הראשון לוקח עד 10 דקות.

2. **הגדירו אזור זמן ומטבע** (Set your timezone and currency) — עברו להגדרות, בחרו Asia/Jerusalem ו-ILS כמטבע ברירת מחדל.

3. **הגדירו COGS לפי SKU** (Set COGS per SKU) — עברו לדף הרווחיות (Profit) והזינו את עלות הסחורה לכל מוצר. זה הצעד הכי חשוב לדוחות הרווח האמיתי.

4. **הזמינו חברי צוות** (Invite teammates) — אם יש לכם מנהל/ת תוכן, מדיה ממומנת, או פיננסים — הוסיפו אותם מהגדרות > Users.

5. **חברו מודעות Meta** (Connect Meta Ads) — עברו ל-Integrations, לחצו על Meta Ads, והתחברו עם חשבון הפרסום שלכם. זה מפעיל את ה-attribution.

6. **הפעילו את סיכום ה-AI השבועי** (Enable the weekly AI digest) — עברו להגדרות > Weekly Digest, הכניסו את האימייל שלכם, ובחרו את יום ושעת השליחה (מומלץ: ראשון בבוקר).

7. **בדקו את הסיכום הראשון ביום שני הקרוב** (Review your first digest next Monday) — הסיכום ישלח אוטומטית. קראו אותו ושמרו שאלות/תגובות לשיחת הפידבק השבועית.

---

**Owner note:** Steps 3 (COGS input) and 5 (Meta connect) are the highest-leverage onboarding actions and the most likely drop-off points. Consider building an onboarding checklist UI inside the app, or an email nudge sequence via Resend, that prompts the customer 24h after signup if these two steps are not completed.
